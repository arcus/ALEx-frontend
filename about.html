ALEx : Arcus Learning Exchange

The Arcus Learning Exchange (ALEx) is an open-source platform developed at the Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia. Using a modular learning paradigm and open-source education, ALEx provides each learner an individualized curriculum based on their own goals. ALEx also provides an interactive computational environment where learners can write and execute code in a variety of languages (including R, Python, and SQL).


ALEx currently includes several dozen lessons, from importing data to training neural networks. The ALEx platform also encourages instructors and domain experts to donate their own lessons - for instance, experts at the Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia contributed lessons in genomics and high performance computing. Our roster of lessons is continuously expanding, and you can help!


Key platform features


• Learners submit curriculum requests based on their learning goals and project needs, and receive an individual curriculum

• A curriculum is composed of lessons, which run interactively on the ALEx platform

• Instructors can contribute lessons (as .rmd files) and map learner goals to lessons

• The platform leverages the security and feature-set of REDCap, shiny-server, and learnr


Our build is currently in beta. Thank you for your patience as we experience a few limitations to usage - for instance, consider limiting your use of ALEx to one or two tabs of lessons at a time.
