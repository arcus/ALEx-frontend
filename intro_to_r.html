Arcus is an initiative by the Children’s Hospital of Philadelphia (CHOP) Research Institute, which aims to connect clinical and research data. We allow biomedical researchers to conduct highly innovative, data-driven, computationally complex, and reproducible research. We join researchers in collaborative endeavors that promote science and scientists and we stand at the forefront of new research practices that promote science that is transparent, ambitious, and nimble.

Lesson 1:  Variables

It sounds like you are somewhat familiar with R and RStudio but want to become more adept at statistics. Below is a curriculum for you that takes you through some statistical tests, their applicability and how to carry them out in R.


1. Please inspect your packet. It contains Hadley Wickham’s seminal article about “tidy data” (a concept that is at the heart of good coding in R), which you can read at your leisure, and numerous cheat sheets that are loosely arranged from least to most advanced, to which you can refer as you follow the instructions below.

2. Download a copy of John McDonald's Handbook of Biologic Statistics. This is an excellent resource to refer to when you need more background on a specific subject.

Here is a list of lessons we chose based on your stated goals. They can be completed in any modern browser. Your progress is saved and you can return at any time.
